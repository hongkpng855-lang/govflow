3️⃣ Step-by-Step Registration Process 註冊流程

I've done this process myself multiple times. Here's exactly what happens: 我自己搞過幾次，以下係實際流程：

Step 1: Choose Your Company Name 揀公司名

  • English name (required) + Chinese name (optional but recommended for local business)
  • Must not be identical to any existing company on the Companies Register
  • Check name availability for free on the eCRIS website
  • Reserve the name at the same time as incorporation (HK$1,295 per name check)

Step 2: Prepare Documents 準備文件

  • Articles of Association (AA): Can use standard model AA (free) or custom AA
  • Incorporation Form (NNC1): Details of company name, registered office address, directors, shareholders, and secretary
  • Business Registration (BR) Application: Filed simultaneously with Inland Revenue Department
⚠️ 親身提醒： If you're from a non-English-speaking country, you'll need a certified translation of your passport and proof of address. Make sure the translator is qualified and the certification is acceptable in Hong Kong. I once had a client whose Russian notary translation was rejected — cost him 2 extra weeks.

Step 3: Submit to Companies Registry 提交公司註冊處

  • Can be done online via eCRIS (electronic form) or paper-based
  • Processing time: eCRIS ≈ 1-2 hours (instant for simple cases), paper ≈ 4-7 working days
  • Government fees:
    • Incorporation fee: HK$1,545
    • Business Registration fee: HK$2,250 (1-year certificate) or HK$5,750 (3-year certificate) — as of 2026

Step 4: Receive Your Documents 收取文件

Once approved, you'll receive:

  • Certificate of Incorporation (CI) — your company's "birth certificate"
  • Business Registration Certificate (BR) — tax registration
  • Articles of Association (AA) — certified copy
  • Company stamp (chop) if ordered

Total time from Step 1 to receiving documents: 1-3 working days (if done electronically).

✅ Quick fact: A Hong Kong company can be fully registered in under 24 hours with eCRIS. I've done it in 3 hours before. The BR takes another 2 days to issue but you can start operations right away.

4️⃣ No Hong Kong Address? Here's Your Solution 冇香港地址點搞？

This is the #1 question I get from foreign founders. Every Hong Kong company must have a registered office address in Hong Kong. If you're not physically in HK, here are your options:

Option A: Virtual Office / Service Address (Recommended)

  • Cost: HK$1,500 - HK$6,000 per year
  • Includes: Registered address, mail forwarding, sometimes phone/ fax services
  • Providers: Regus, Servcorp, The Executive Centre, many local secretarial firms
  • My recommendation: Use a reputable provider. I tried a HK$800/year "cheap address" once — lost 3 government letters and almost got my company struck off.

Option B: Company Secretary's Address

  • Many licensed company secretaries include their office address in the incorporation package
  • Cost: Usually bundled — total incorporation package HK$3,000 - HK$8,000
  • Make sure they forward all government correspondence promptly

Option C: Coworking Space Address

  • Some coworking spaces allow you to use their address as your registered office
  • Risk: If you leave the coworking space, you must update the address with Companies Registry
  • I don't recommend this for long-term — I've seen too many people lose track of address changes
🚨 我中過嘅伏 — Lost Government Letters: I used a cheap virtual office that cost HK$800/year. They received my Companies Registry letter about an SCR spot check but "forgot" to forward it to me. By the time I found out (through a reminder from my secretary), I'd missed the deadline. HK$8,000 fine for something that should have cost HK$80/month in proper service. Don't be cheap on your registered address, seriously. 千祈唔好慳註冊地址嘅錢，我試過為咗慳每月百幾蚊，最後罰咗八千蚊。

8️⃣ Common Pitfalls — Mistakes I've Made 常見陷阱（我搞過）

Pitfall 1: Choosing the Cheapest Company Secretary

I've been burned by this. A HK$1,500/year secretary service sounds great until they don't file your NAR1 on time, and you get a HK$1,000+ late filing penalty. The cheapest options often outsource everything and have no accountability. Pay HK$4,000-6,000/year for a reputable secretary — it's worth every dollar.

Pitfall 2: Thinking You Can Do Everything Remotely

You CAN incorporate remotely. You CAN'T easily open a bank account remotely (with traditional banks). Most banks still require at least one director to visit HK in person. Plan a trip to HK specifically for bank opening. Don't assume it'll happen automatically.

Pitfall 3: Ignoring the Audit Requirement

All Hong Kong companies must prepare audited financial statements. Yes, even if your company has zero activity. The first audit can be done up to 18 months after incorporation, but you must plan for it. I've seen foreigners panic-buy auditors at 3x the normal price because they left it too late.

🚨 另一個教訓 — Offshore Claim Pitfall: Many people are told "Hong Kong doesn't tax offshore income." That's true, but you need to file an offshore claim with supporting evidence. It's not automatic. I once had a client who assumed all his trading income was offshore. The IRD assessed him for 3 years back. Interest + penalties added up to over HK$150,000. Get professional advice before assuming offshore treatment applies. 如果你想做離岸收入申報，千祈唔好自己亂估，一定要搵會計師處理，否則被追討過去幾年嘅稅款加罰款，真係好大件事。

Pitfall 4: Not Understanding the Director/Shareholder Distinction

As a foreign founder, you can be the sole director AND sole shareholder. But if you want to protect your assets, consider having at least one other shareholder or using a holding company structure. Also: you can appoint a nominee director if you want privacy, but this adds cost and compliance complexity.

Pitfall 5: Not Having a Local Bank Account for Daily Operations

A multi-currency account with Wise or Airwallex is useful, but many clients and suppliers still prefer to pay into a "real" HK bank account. Don't rely solely on fintech platforms — maintain at least one traditional bank account alongside your digital accounts.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions 常見問題

Can a foreigner be 100% shareholder and director of a Hong Kong company?

Yes. Hong Kong company law does not require any local directors or shareholders. A foreign individual can be the sole director and 100% shareholder. There is no nationality or residency requirement for directors or shareholders of a private limited company.

點解外國人喺香港開公司咁受歡迎？

香港係少數容許100%外資持有嘅司法管轄區，冇最低資本要求、冇外匯管制、稅率低（首200萬利潤僅8.25%）、公司法例英文友善，而且註冊只需1-3日。對於做國際貿易、電商、金融科技嘅創業者嚟講，香港係進入亞洲市場嘅最佳跳板。

How long does it take to register a Hong Kong company?

If you use the electronic system (eCRIS), the incorporation itself takes 1-2 hours after submission. However, you should allow 1-3 working days to receive the physical Certificate of Incorporation and Business Registration certificate. The fastest I've ever done it is 3 hours from start to receiving the e-certificate.

我需要親身去香港註冊公司嗎？

不需要。你可以完全遙距註冊香港公司。所有文件可以網上提交，公司秘書可以幫你處理政府文件。但開銀行戶口方面，大部分傳統銀行仍然需要董事親身到香港面見。虛擬銀行同 fintech 平台（如 Airwallex、Statrys）可能可以遙距開戶。

What happens if I don't file annual returns?

Late filing of Annual Return (NAR1) incurs a penalty ranging from HK$870 to HK$3,480 (depending on how late). If you don't file for several years, the Companies Registry can strike off your company and the directors can be personally fined up to HK$50,000. Your company secretary should be handling this — if they don't, change them immediately.

Is it mandatory to have audited accounts in Hong Kong?

Yes, all Hong Kong companies are required by law to have their annual financial statements audited by a Certified Public Accountant (CPA), regardless of turnover. Companies with profit under HK$2M, or dormant companies, may qualify for a simplified reporting regime, but they still need an audit report and tax return filing. The only exception is companies that are wholly dormant with no transactions at all.

Can I use a virtual address for company registration?

Yes, you can use a virtual office address as your registered office address. However, the address must be a physical Hong Kong address (not a PO Box), and you must be able to receive government correspondence there. Many company secretaries provide a registered address as part of their service package. Just make sure they actually forward your mail — I learned this the hard way.

What is a "Company Secretary" and do I need one?

Yes, every HK company MUST appoint a Company Secretary. It's a legal requirement under the Companies Ordinance. The secretary can be an individual (must be HK resident) or a corporate body (with a HK business address). They handle compliance filings, maintain statutory records, and ensure the company meets its legal obligations. For foreign-owned companies, a professional corporate secretary is essential.
